Framed Interior Mirror

2021-2022

Led mechanical design for an interior auto-dimming mirror for multiple vehicles from various OEM's, with the glass framed by the plastic of the rear housing. Patent-pending new construction.

Development of a new interior rearview mirror, launching an injection molded rear housing and internal plate, two laser-cut glass elements, a die-cut foam tape, a cast mount, and a new PCB. This product is a brand new construction for interior mirrors, which required extensive initial DFMEA work, tolerance stackups, and the use of prototype injection molding tools. These tools enabled early learning to prove out this new construction in both its' assembly method and vehicle performance. Along with design reviews internal to Gentex, extensive reviews were done with the OEM (via video call) to review and confirm the design.

This product included innovation mechanically, electrically, and in the manufacturing space. Because of this multifaceted nature, I had constant collaboration with the above teams, as well as program and project management. Many of the validation tests for this product are intended to simulate a vehicle lifetime, and so, even when accelerated, can have long lead times. This meant that there was little room for error in the product timeline. This product has successfully completed validation and is in final manufacturing trials.
