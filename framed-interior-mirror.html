Framed Interior Mirror

2020-2021

Led mechanical design for a framed interior auto-dimming mirror for multiple vehicles from various OEM's. 'Framed' indicates the glass is surrounded by the plastic of the rear housing. Patent-pending new construction.

This development of a new interior rearview mirror included launching: 1. an injection molded rear housing, 2. an injection molded internal plate, 3. two laser-cut glass elements, 4. a die-cut foam tape, 5. a cast mount, and 6. a new PCB. This product is a brand new construction for interior mirrors, which required extensive initial DFMEA work, tolerance stackups, and the use of prototype injection molding tools. These tools enabled early learning to prove out this new construction in both its assembly method and vehicle performance. Along with design reviews internal to Gentex, extensive reviews were done with the OEM (via conference call) to review and confirm the design.

Shown on the right is a modal analysis conducted for this mirror. This finds the principal resonant frequencies for the mirror, enabling a prediction of how well the rearview image will resist "blurring" due to road or acoustical vibrations. The new construction of this mirror allows for excellent vibrational performance.
Beyond the mechanical innovation leading to better vibration performance (and other improvements), this product also had innovation in its electronic and manufacturing aspects. Because of this multifaceted project nature, I had constant cross-functional collaboration with the above teams, as well as program and project management. Many of the validation tests for this product are intended to simulate a vehicle lifetime, and so, even when accelerated, have long lead times. This meant that there was little room for error in the product timeline. Nonetheless, this product has successfully completed validation on time and is in final manufacturing trials.
