Lighted Blind Spot Module

2019-2021

Led mechanical design for a lighted blind spot module, currently being implemented on 3 new vehicles. Significant performance, manufacturing, and sourcing improvements over the previous generation of modules.

Developed a new lighted blind spot module (BSM), launching an injection molded and metallized optical component, a foam/optical component, and a PCB. A BSM System Overview can be found here. For the foam/optical component, I was significantly involved in the choice of the optical diffuser, coordinating constantly with our optics team to select a diffusing film that balanced optical performance, LED light output, and resistance to environmental factors. I also provided a PCB manufacturing concept that was the inspiration for the final manufactured PCB design and significantly increased end throughput.

Because of the optical nature of this project, the set of tools available in the "Part" modeling module of CATIA was insufficient. I branched out into the "Generative Shape Design" module and gained experience in surfacing. The optics for this design went through several iterations to improve light footprint, so it was necessary to have the CATIA tree organized and correctly linked so small updates would immediately cascade through the part without errors.
